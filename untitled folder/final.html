Summary of the Dataset

This dataset includes all the confirmed hate crime incidents recorded by the NYPD that have taken place between the years of 2019 to 2022 in the state of New York. The dataset is very detailed having columns spanning from all the minority groups targeted, to the offense that was committed towards them, to the motive behind the crime. Out of the minority groups targeted, the Jewish community has been victimized the most over the past three years.

This is newsworthy because antisemitism is rising in the United States. It’s becoming a major issue and is starting to run rapidly from schools to the workplace. Social media definitely has contributed to this problem as communication outlets like Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Reddit and other platforms have allowed users to say whatever is on their mind while hiding behind their screens without any consequences. Findings

My first visual is a grouped bars chart of all the religious groups that have been targeted between 2019 to 2022 and the amount of recorded hate crimes each of them faced in New York. Diving deeper into the numbers and the data itself, it is noted that the Jewish population has been targeted 866 times. This is 43.97% percent of the total amount of hate crimes that have been recorded from all the religions!

Luke Tress of the Times of Israel also reported more on these numbers. He stated, “The rise in hate crimes in New York corresponds with nationwide trends. The ADL recorded 2,717 antisemitic incidents across the country in 2021, a 34% increase from the previous year, and the highest since it began tracking in 1979.”

More Insight of the Issue

My next visual is a column chart that examines the most common types of hate crimes recorded against the Jewish community between 2019 to 2022. The top three crimes committed were assault and related offenses, offenses against public ordinance and criminal mischief and offenses related to it. Criminal Mischief was by far the most common hate crime committed with 217 different recorded events. According to NewYorkLawyers.org, in the state of New York, Criminal Mischief involves hate crimes in which the perpetrator would recklessly damage the property of the victim without consent.

To former NYPD police officer Anthony Alesia this isn’t shocking as he has dealt with multiple mischief related incidents throughout his career. “I saw multiple swastikas spray painted on people’s fences.” Alesia also talked about a common motive people have in mind when committing hate crimes. He stated, “ I saw a lot of hate crimes based on money and jealousy and hurting people who are doing well.” Outside of committing an offense out of hatred towards a specific religion Alesia’s take paints a clearer picture as to why criminals target certain groups.
